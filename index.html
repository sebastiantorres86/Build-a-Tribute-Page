Lawrence Tesler

The UI pioneer responsible for cut, copy, and paste

Tesler grew up in New York and graduated from the Bronx High School of Science in 1961. He attended Stanford University, where he studied Computer Science in the 1960s, and worked for a time at the Stanford Artificial Intelligence Laboratory.

He contributed in 1967 with Douglas Engelbart in the development for the Palo Alto Research Center (PARC) of Xerox a graphical user interface with mouse, creating the system for sending orders to a computer from a peripheral, which revolutionized computing to power Copy text fragments and paste them back into the text. In 1980 Steve Jobs hired Tesler for Apple, three years later in 1983 Lisa was developed, a mouse Macintosh that was commercially launched by the American company in 1984. Along with Tim Mott was the creator of the Gypsy word processor known for the functions "copy", "cut" and "paste" of the operating systems for which it was recognized.

Over seventeen years he developed the AppleNet projects in which he developed Bill Atkinson's QuickTime, AppleScript and Hypercard software, worked on Apple's Mac computers and designed the Amazon shopping experience. In 2009 he joined the Yahoo consulting team.

He died at the age of seventy-four and the news was announced through the Xerox Corporation's Twitter account without knowing the circumstances of the death.
